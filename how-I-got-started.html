How I Got Started

I suppose it's in my genes. All of my family is artistic, so I was encouraged from a very young age to be creative.

Grade School Years

As a little kid, I used to make "stationery" and write nice things on it and give it to my friends and family.

You're so Thoughtful!

Of course, everyone thought it was adorable that I would draw cute pictures and write sweet things to the people I loved, and so it encouraged me to continue.

Christmas Success!

One year, my mom got me really cool rubber stamps, embossing powder, and heavyweight blank cards and asked me to make her Christmas cards for the season. Of course I jumped at the idea! I made about 100 cards, and they were a huge hit with all of the recipients.

High School Creativity

When I got to high school, I kept making cards. They got more sophisticated as my artistic nature grew. As I collected more craft supplies over the years, my cards kept getting more sophisticated and intricate. People kept telling me, "You should sell these!" And that's where it all started.

Break Time

When I got to college, I quit my little business.

Break Time

I got lost in daily life... College classes, working part-time, and socializing with friends.

Break Time

However I still sent out my own handmade Christmas cards every year.

Break Time

My favorite hobby became a tradition I kept up with for only one season a year.

Studie Stationery

Fast forward 10 years, and I was still casually making holiday cards and sending them to my friends and family. I love holidays and try to celebrate them every season. I began to send out homemade cards for all of the major holidays: Christmas, Valentine's Day, St. Patrick's Day, Easter, Halloween, and Thanksgiving. My friends all loved the thoughtful notes I would send them, so I decided to start hosting craft parties so that my friends could do the same for their loved ones. We had a great time! Socializing, laughing, and sharing creativity can be so much fun!

Studie Stationery Today

One day in 2016, I decided to sell my handmade holiday cards online. Thus started my little shop on Etsy. I put up dozens of types of cards - for all major holidays and also cards to say "thank you," "congratulations," "happy birthday," etc.

I still host craft parties and charge only the cost of the materials used.
