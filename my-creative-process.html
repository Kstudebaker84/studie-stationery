My Creative Process

No, I don't look at Pinterest for inspiration. I go to craft stores. When I shop for card-making supplies, I end up spending a lot of time in each department, looking for unique crafting elements to embellish my cards with. Often times, I use unusual craft elements that aren't in the paper-crafting section of the craft store.

I like mixing rubber stamping/embossing with lace, ribbons, buttons, felt, and fabric. This year, for example, for my Halloween cards, I didn't like the Halloween embellishment selection at ANY craft store, so instead, I bought a huge hole punch and made spiders out of thick black paper and paired it with leftover supplies that I've been collecting for past few decades.

Shopping Inspirations

I start my holiday card shopping and craft ideas sometimes a year or two before I actually create a design. I like buying craft supplies when they go on sale at the end of the season, and then I pair them with the next few years' themes.

I shop for paper-crafting supplies in the card-making section of the craft store (obviously), but then I also shop in the ribbon, button, painting, beading, and kids' sections for a full multi-media look.
